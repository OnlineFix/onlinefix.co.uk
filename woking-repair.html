Serving Woking from our Guildford workshop

OnlineFix is an independent electronics repair shop in Guildford, Surrey, run by Tomas. Customers from Woking and the wider GU21–GU24 area drop their phones, laptops, PCs, MacBooks, tablets and gaming consoles off with us for same-day repairs, board-level micro-soldering and data recovery.

The workshop is roughly 7 miles south of Woking town centre. Most customers drive down via the A322 and A320, or come via the A3 corridor from the station area. Drop-off is by appointment, so you're not waiting in a queue when you arrive — we book a slot, you hand the device over, and we get to work.

Why drive from Woking instead of going local

Most Woking high-street phone shops are screen-and-battery resellers. Anything more complex — a console with a damaged HDMI port, a MacBook with logic-board liquid damage, a hard drive that won't mount — gets sub-contracted out, sometimes shipped to a third party. That means the device leaves Woking anyway, just with less control and a longer turnaround.

OnlineFix does board-level work in-house. Tomas has eight years of experience with micro-soldering, BGA reflow, and the kind of repairs other shops decline. You drop the device with one technician, get a transparent quote, and pick up a working device with a 90-day warranty.

FAQ for Woking customers

Do you collect from Woking?

We're a small workshop with one technician, so we don't run a regular collection service. Drop-off at GU1 4PD is the norm. For high-value or repeat business customers we can occasionally arrange collection — ask when you call.

Can I post my device from Woking?

Yes, most laptops and consoles can be posted in. Get in touch first so we can confirm the repair is viable before you ship. We'll send you packing guidance and a tracked-return option.

What postcodes do you cover for Woking?

All of GU21, GU22, GU23 and GU24 — Woking town centre, Horsell, Knaphill, Goldsworth Park, West Byfleet, Pyrford, Old Woking, Mayford, Brookwood and the surrounding villages. Address is the same: customers come to our Guildford workshop.

Are you cheaper than Currys / Apple / a manufacturer repair?

Almost always, especially for out-of-warranty devices. Apple and most manufacturers will quote a flat-rate replacement; we quote an actual repair. For older devices the saving can be substantial — a £600 MacBook logic-board reflow vs. a £1,200 Apple board swap is a typical example.
